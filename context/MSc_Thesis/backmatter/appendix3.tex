\section{Behavioural Excerpts} \label{ap3:excerpts}

The two transcripts below show examples of irrational behaviours. Excerpt~\ref{ex:buysell-below-cost} shows a seller pricing its object far below production cost, and Excerpt~\ref{ex:trading-self-concession} shows a Trading negotiator conceding more than its opponent even demanded.

\begin{negotiationexcerpt}[label=ex:buysell-below-cost]{A seller prices its object far below production cost}{BuySell, 12--14B tier, Gemma~3 12B seller vs.\ Qwen3 14B; run \texttt{1775029333404}}
\speaker{P1 (Gemma 3 12B, seller)} Hello Player BLUE, I'm offering my X for 5 ZUP. Let me know what you think!\\
\texttt{<newly proposed trade> Player RED Gives X: 1 | Player BLUE Gives ZUP: 5 </newly proposed trade>}

\medskip
\speaker{P2 (Qwen3 14B, buyer)} I accept your offer of 5 ZUP for X. It's a great deal!\\
\texttt{<player answer> ACCEPT </player answer>}

\medskip
\noindent The object cost the seller 40 ZUP to produce, so opening at 5 ZUP guarantees a loss before negotiation even begins. The buyer accepts immediately: seller surplus $-35$, buyer surplus $+55$.
\end{negotiationexcerpt}

\begin{negotiationexcerpt}[label=ex:trading-self-concession]{A negotiator concedes more than its opponent even demanded}{Trading, 24--27B tier, Gemma~3 27B (Player RED) vs.\ Mistral-Small 3.2 24B; run \texttt{1775875077879}}
\speaker{P1 (Gemma 3 27B)} Let's try to make a deal that benefits us both in this final round.\\
\texttt{<newly proposed trade> Player RED Gives X: 15 | Player BLUE Gives Y: 3 </newly proposed trade>}

\medskip
\speaker{P2 (Mistral, counter)} I appreciate the offer, but I'm interested in maximizing my resources. Let's see if we can find a better deal.\\
\texttt{<newly proposed trade> Player RED Gives X: 20 | Player BLUE Gives Y: 5 </newly proposed trade>}

\medskip
\speaker{P1 (Gemma 3 27B)} I understand you want to maximize your resources. Let's try to find a middle ground that works for both of us.\\
\texttt{<newly proposed trade> Player RED Gives X: 22 | Player BLUE Gives Y: 5 </newly proposed trade>}

\medskip
\speaker{P2 (Mistral)} Thank you for the improved offer. I accept this trade.\\
\texttt{<player answer> ACCEPT </player answer>}

\medskip
\noindent Both resources are equally valuable, so this is a zero-sum exchange. Gemma's ``middle ground'' gives away 22 X for only 5 Y (two more X than Mistral itself had asked for in the previous turn) for a net loss of 17 units.
\end{negotiationexcerpt}
